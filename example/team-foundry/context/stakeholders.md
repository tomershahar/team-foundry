# Stakeholders

*These are the people outside the product trio who have a stake in what we're building or how we're doing.*

## Internal stakeholders

**Ren Watanabe — CEO**
What she actually watches: ARR growth, churn, and NPS. She trusts the team on tactics but cares deeply about whether customers are succeeding.
How she prefers updates: a brief written update before the monthly leadership review. No more than one page. She wants to know: are we on track on outcomes, and are there any surprises?
What sets her off: finding out about a problem from a customer before hearing it from the team.
What she's particularly watching this quarter: whether the exception UI redesign moves NPS. She's skeptical of UI projects that don't show up in metrics.

**Joonatan Mäkinen — Head of Sales**
What he actually watches: sales velocity, the "Clearline can't do X" objection rate, and win/loss reasons.
How he prefers updates: a standing 30-min sync with Sarah every two weeks, plus a Slack note when a feature ships that affects a current deal.
What sets him off: hearing from a prospect that a competitor has a feature we don't, when he didn't know we were considering it.
Ask from the product team: give him a one-sentence heads-up on anything that changes the competitive story, before it ships.

**Amara Diallo — Head of Customer Success**
What she actually watches: NPS, time-to-resolution for support tickets, and churn signals.
How she prefers updates: included in design reviews for any feature that changes the AP lead's core workflow. She doesn't want to learn about workflow changes when customers start emailing her.
What sets her off: shipping something that generates a wave of "how do I..." support tickets.
Ask from the product team: 2 weeks notice before anything in the approval or exception workflow changes.

## External stakeholders

**Mira Halonen — AP Lead, Kestrel Logistics (design partner)**
What she watches: whether the exception UI actually helps her trust the system.
Engagement: monthly session with Sarah and Leo. Currently active on exception UI v2 designs.
What she's told us: "I don't need the software to be smart. I need it to show me what it's thinking so I can trust it."

**James Okafor — Finance Manager, Verdant Supply Co.**
What he watches: whether approval fatigue improves after the routing change.
Engagement: quarterly check-in with Sarah. Not a design partner — a customer we monitor closely.

## Board / investors

Not directly engaged with the product team. Sarah provides a quarterly product update to Ren who shares with the board. No direct product team-to-board communication.
