# Outcomes — Q2 2026

## Committed outcomes

**O1 — AP leads at SMB customers process month-end in under 2 days**
Baseline: 4.1 days average. Target: ≤2 days for customers with <200 invoices/month.
Signal: we'll see this in time-to-close data from customers who use the new approval routing.
Why it matters: month-end duration is the #1 complaint in NPS verbatims this quarter.

**O2 — New customers reach their first auto-approved invoice within 5 days of going live**
Baseline: 6.2 days. Target: ≤5 days.
Signal: time-to-first-value in onboarding funnel (tracked per cohort).
Why it matters: customers who hit first value fast have 3× better 90-day retention.

**O3 — Finance managers trust the exception queue enough to stop spot-checking approvals**
Baseline: qualitative (customer interviews show 6/10 still manually verify auto-approvals).
Target: 8/10 customers say they no longer spot-check in post-launch interview.
Signal: we'll measure this via a 30-day post-adoption interview for customers on the new exception UI.
Why it matters: if they're spot-checking, we haven't solved the trust problem — we've just moved the work.

## Stretch outcome

**O4 — One enterprise customer (200+ invoices/month) reaches 80% auto-processing rate**
This requires the multi-approver workflow we've been deferring. We'll only pursue it if O1–O3 are on track by mid-quarter.

## What we're not working toward this quarter

- Reducing support ticket volume (we're watching it, but it's a consequence, not a target)
- International invoice formats (deferred; only 3 customers have asked)
- Mobile app (not enough demand signal to justify the context-switch cost)
