## How we make decisions

**Product decisions (what to build, in what order):** Sarah decides. Marcus and Leo are consulted and can escalate disagreements within 48 hours. If escalated, we discuss in the next trio sync and decide together.

**Technical decisions (how to build it):** Marcus decides, with Priya as the technical lead for implementation details. Sarah is informed. Architecture decisions go into `engineering/decisions/`.

**Design decisions (how it looks and feels):** Leo decides, with Emma on execution. Sarah reviews for outcome alignment. Marcus reviews for technical feasibility before designs are finalized.

**Anything crossing all three domains:** We discuss in the weekly trio sync and need explicit agreement from all three to move forward.

## How we handle disagreement

We name the disagreement explicitly — "I think we're making the wrong call here because X" — and give 24 hours for async response before scheduling a sync. We do not let disagreements fester silently. We do not ship something one of us has unresolved concerns about unless the timeline genuinely forces it, in which case we document the concern and revisit it within the quarter.

## Constraints on each other

- Sarah will not add scope to a sprint in flight without a conversation with Marcus.
- Marcus will not make architectural decisions that affect product behavior without a conversation with Sarah.
- Leo will not finalize designs that haven't been reviewed for technical feasibility.

## Working cadence

- **Weekly trio sync:** Monday 10:00 (60 min). Review outcomes progress, surface blockers, align on priorities.
- **Sprint planning:** Every two weeks, Tuesday. Marcus + Priya run it. Sarah and Leo join for first 30 min.
- **Design reviews:** On demand. Leo schedules with Sarah and Marcus when designs are at "ready for feedback" stage.
- **Customer calls:** Sarah owns scheduling. Leo joins when it's a research session. Marcus joins when it's a technical discussion.
